Our Story

Three generations of Hawaiian beekeeping

In 1962, my grandfather placed his first hive on the slopes of Mauna Kea. He wasn't trying to start a business — he just loved the bees. He'd spend hours watching them work, learning their patterns, understanding their needs.

Today, we tend over 200 hives across the Big Island. Each location is chosen for the unique flora that surrounds it — from rare Lehua blossoms to wild macadamia flowers. We never heat our honey. We never ultra-filter it. What comes out of the hive is what goes into the jar.

This isn't artisanal as a marketing term. This is honey made the slow way, the only way we know how.

"The bees don't hurry. Neither do we."

How We Work

Slow by design

Step One

Harvest

We visit each hive by hand, selecting only frames that are fully capped. Never rushed, never forced. The bees tell us when they're ready.

Step Two

Extract

Cold-extracted in our Waimea honey house. No heat, no pressure. The honey flows at its own pace, preserving every enzyme and flavor compound.

Step Three

Jar

Hand-poured into glass jars within 48 hours of extraction. Each jar is dated and traceable to the exact hive and harvest date.
